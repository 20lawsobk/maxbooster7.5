# Acceptable Use Policy

**Document Version:** 1.0  
**Effective Date:** November 11, 2025  
**Last Reviewed:** November 11, 2025  
**Policy Owner:** Chief Information Security Officer  
**Compliance Standards:** ISO 27001 (A.8), SOC2

## 1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) defines appropriate use of Max Booster's information systems, networks, data, and technology resources. All users must comply with this policy to protect company assets and maintain security.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all:
- Employees (full-time, part-time, temporary, contractors)
- Max Booster systems, networks, devices, and data
- Company-provided and personal devices accessing company resources

## 3. General Use Principles

### 3.1 Authorized Use
- Systems may only be used for legitimate business purposes
- Access limited to authorized individuals with valid business need
- Personal use permitted within reasonable limits and compliance with policy
- No expectation of privacy when using company systems

### 3.2 Account Security
- User credentials are confidential and must not be shared
- Strong passwords required (minimum 12 characters, complexity requirements)
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) enabled for all accounts
- Suspicious activity must be reported immediately to security team

### 3.3 Data Handling
- Company data must be protected according to classification level
- Confidential and Restricted data requires encryption in transit and at rest
- No unauthorized copying, downloading, or transmitting of company data
- Data loss prevention (DLP) controls must not be circumvented

## 4. Prohibited Activities

Users must NOT:

### 4.1 Security Violations
- Bypass or disable security controls, firewalls, or monitoring systems
- Attempt unauthorized access to systems, networks, or data
- Share credentials or allow unauthorized individuals to access systems
- Install unauthorized software, applications, or browser extensions
- Connect unauthorized devices to the network

### 4.2 Malicious Activities
- Introduce malware, viruses, or other malicious code
- Launch denial-of-service attacks or network disruptions
- Engage in port scanning, vulnerability scanning, or penetration testing without authorization
- Exploit security vulnerabilities for any purpose

### 4.3 Data Misuse
- Access, copy, or transmit confidential data without authorization
- Disclose sensitive information to unauthorized parties
- Use company data for personal gain or competitive advantage
- Violate data privacy laws (GDPR, CCPA, etc.)

### 4.4 Inappropriate Content
- Access, store, or transmit offensive, pornographic, or discriminatory content
- Harass, threaten, or defame individuals via company systems
- Engage in illegal activities or violate intellectual property rights

### 4.5 Resource Misuse
- Use systems for cryptocurrency mining or excessive personal use
- Consume excessive bandwidth or storage without business justification
- Interfere with other users' legitimate use of systems

## 5. Email and Communications

### 5.1 Email Use
- Email is company property and may be monitored
- Professional tone and content required in business communications
- Phishing and suspicious emails must be reported to security
- Sensitive data must be encrypted when sent externally

### 5.2 External Communications
- Company information may only be disclosed with proper authorization
- Social media posts must not reveal confidential business information
- Public statements about the company require executive approval

## 6. Remote Access and BYOD

### 6.1 Remote Access
- VPN required for all remote connections to company network
- Public Wi-Fi requires VPN and security precautions
- Remote work environments must maintain reasonable security

### 6.2 Personal Devices (BYOD)
- Personal devices accessing company data must meet security requirements
- Mobile device management (MDM) may be required
- Company reserves right to remotely wipe corporate data from personal devices
- Lost or stolen devices must be reported immediately

## 7. Monitoring and Privacy

### 7.1 System Monitoring
- Company reserves the right to monitor all system activity
- Logs and audit trails maintained for security and compliance
- No expectation of privacy on company systems
- Monitoring conducted in compliance with applicable laws

### 7.2 Privacy Protections
- Monitoring activities comply with GDPR and local privacy laws
- Personal data collection limited to legitimate business purposes
- Employee privacy rights respected within legal framework

## 8. Software and Intellectual Property

### 8.1 Licensed Software
- Only properly licensed software may be installed
- Software licenses must be tracked and maintained
- Pirated or unlicensed software strictly prohibited

### 8.2 Intellectual Property
- Company owns all work product created using company resources
- Open source software use must comply with licensing requirements
- Third-party intellectual property must be properly licensed

## 9. Incident Reporting

Users must immediately report:
- Security incidents or suspected breaches
- Lost or stolen devices containing company data
- Suspected malware infections
- Phishing attempts or social engineering attacks
- Policy violations or suspicious activity

**Reporting Contact:** security@maxbooster.com

## 10. Policy Violations

### 10.1 Consequences
Violations may result in:
- Verbal or written warning
- Suspension of system access
- Termination of employment
- Legal action and criminal prosecution
- Financial liability for damages

### 10.2 Investigation
- All reported violations will be investigated
- Users must cooperate with security investigations
- Systems and data may be forensically examined

## 11. Policy Acknowledgment

All users must:
- Review and acknowledge this policy annually
- Complete security awareness training
- Immediately report violations or concerns

## 12. Policy Review

This policy is reviewed annually and updated as needed to address:
- New technologies and business practices
- Emerging security threats
- Regulatory changes
- Lessons learned from incidents
